Frequently asked

Honest answers, upfront.

How do authorized-user (AU) tradelines actually work?
When you become an authorized user on someone's seasoned credit account, that account's history (age, limit, payment record) reports onto your credit file at the next statement closing date. AU tradelines do not give you spending access — you receive no card and cannot use the account. The benefit is exclusively to your credit profile.
Is buying authorized-user tradelines legal?
Authorized-user reporting is permitted under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (Regulation B), and the act of being added as an AU is legal. However, lenders may consider AU accounts less favorably during manual underwriting if they suspect the relationship is not bona fide. We disclose this fully — AU tradelines are a credit-profile tool, not a loan-approval guarantee, and you should not use this service if you intend to deceive a lender during a mortgage or large loan application.
Do you guarantee a specific score increase?
No — and any company that guarantees a specific score increase is violating the Credit Repair Organizations Act. Score changes depend on your existing file, the data furnished, and scoring-model differences across bureaus. We provide good-faith estimates based on similar customer outcomes, but we cannot and will not promise specific results.
What is your dispute process and what can be removed?
We dispute items that are inaccurate, outdated, or unverifiable under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA). This includes items past the 7-year reporting limit, items belonging to someone else, items with incorrect dates or amounts, and items the furnisher cannot verify. We do not promise to remove accurate negative items — federal law does not permit anyone to do that.
What information do you collect and when?
At signup we collect your name, email, phone, state, and ZIP. Sensitive data — full date of birth, Social Security Number, full address, ID documents — is collected only when you enroll in a service that requires it. Your SSN is masked on screen, encrypted in transit using TLS 1.3, and encrypted at rest with AES-256. We never sell your data.
How do I cancel?
Cancel from your billing page at any time, or write to us. Under CROA, you also have a right to cancel any specific credit-repair contract within 3 business days of signing without penalty. Open service work in progress will be completed; future billing stops immediately.

How authorized-user tradelines work
Read this short overview before continuing. It explains exactly what you get, and what you don't.
01
You are added as an authorized user. The cardholder adds your name to a seasoned account. You receive no card. You cannot spend.
02
The account reports to your file. On the next statement closing date, the account's history (age, limit, payment record) appears on your bureau reports.
03
The slot is held for 60 days. After two statement cycles, you are removed. The history may continue to report depending on the bureau.
Important: AU tradelines are a credit-profile tool, not loan-approval certainty. Lenders may scrutinize AU accounts during manual underwriting. Do not use this service to misrepresent your finances on a mortgage or large-loan application. We do not guarantee specific score outcomes.

CROA Disclosure Statement

Federal law requires that we provide this notice to you before any credit repair service contract is executed. Please read it carefully.

Consumer Credit File Rights Under State and Federal Law

You have a right to dispute inaccurate information in your credit report by contacting the credit bureau directly. However, neither you nor any credit repair company has the right to have accurate, current, and verifiable information removed from your credit report. The credit bureau must remove accurate, negative information from your report only if it is over seven years old. Bankruptcy information can be reported for ten years.

You have a right to obtain a copy of your credit report from a credit bureau. You may be charged a reasonable fee. There is no fee, however, if you have been turned down for credit, employment, insurance, or a rental dwelling because of information in your credit report within the preceding sixty days. The credit bureau must provide someone to help you interpret the information in your credit file. You are entitled to receive a free copy of your credit report if you are unemployed and intend to apply for employment in the next sixty days, if you are a recipient of public welfare assistance, or if you have reason to believe that there is inaccurate information in your credit report due to fraud.

You have the right to sue a credit repair organization that violates the Credit Repair Organizations Act. This law prohibits deceptive practices by credit repair organizations.

Your Right to Cancel

You have the right to cancel your contract with any credit repair organization for any reason within 3 business days from the date you signed it.

What TradelineIQ Promises — and Does Not Promise

  • We do not guarantee any specific score outcome. Score changes depend on your existing credit file, the items being disputed or added, and bureau-specific scoring models.
  • We will not request payment for credit repair services until those services have been performed. Per CROA § 1679b(b).
  • We will not knowingly file a dispute we believe to be false or misleading. You confirm at signup that your dispute claims reflect a good-faith belief the information is inaccurate.
  • We will provide you with a written contract before any service is performed, with a clear statement of the services to be performed, the total cost, a 3-day cancellation form, and your rights under CROA.

Authorized-User Tradelines — Special Notice

AU tradelines are not credit-repair services and are not regulated under CROA. However, we apply the same transparency standards: no guaranteed outcomes, full disclosure of the service mechanics, and a 60-day reporting window with documented timelines. You should not use AU tradelines to misrepresent your finances on a current or pending mortgage or large loan application. Some lenders treat AU accounts less favorably during manual underwriting.
